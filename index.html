Welcome to Cyber Crime Awareness

Cyber crime is one of the biggest threats in today’s interconnected world. Whether you're a student, employee, or business owner, you could be at risk from hackers, fraudsters, and data thieves.

What is Cyber Crime?

Cyber crime includes illegal activities that are carried out using computers or digital networks. These crimes range from hacking and identity theft to ransomware attacks and cyberbullying.

Common Cyber Threats

  • Phishing scams and fake emails
  • Malware and viruses
  • Online fraud and scams
  • Hacking and unauthorized access
  • Ransomware attacks

Trends in Cyber Crime

Cyber attacks are becoming more sophisticated. Criminals now use artificial intelligence, deepfakes, and social engineering to trick users and bypass security systems.

According to global reports, cyber crime will cost the world over $10 trillion by 2025. Every individual and organization must take steps to stay safe online.

About Cyber Crime

Impact of Cyber Crime

Cyber crime doesn't just steal money — it can damage reputations, destroy data, and compromise national security. Small businesses, in particular, are frequent targets because of weaker security systems.

Types of Cyber Crimes

  • Hacking: Accessing systems without authorization.
  • Phishing: Using fake emails or websites to trick users into giving personal info.
  • Cyberbullying: Harassment or bullying using digital platforms.
  • Data Breaches: Large-scale exposure of personal or financial data.
  • Cyber Terrorism: Politically motivated attacks on critical systems.

Role of Law Enforcement

Agencies such as the FBI, Interpol, and national cybersecurity task forces investigate and combat cyber threats. However, international cooperation is often needed due to the global nature of the internet.

Prevention Tips

  • Keep your software and operating systems updated.
  • Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication.
  • Don’t click on suspicious links or download unknown files.
  • Back up important data regularly.
  • Use firewalls and antivirus software.
